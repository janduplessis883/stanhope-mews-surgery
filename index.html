Childhood Vaccination FAQ

It is normal to have questions about medication given to your child, and vaccines are no exception. Here are answers to the most common questions.

Why should I have my child vaccinated?

Herd immunity does not protect against all diseases. For example, tetanus is caught from bacteria in the environment rather than from other people.

For herd immunity to work, most people need to be vaccinated. Low vaccination rates in some communities mean unvaccinated children are more likely to encounter others without protection. This allows infectious diseases to spread quickly.

Immune System Capacity

Even a tiny baby's immune system can easily cope with several vaccines at once. From birth, babies come into contact with millions of germs every day.

1/1000th Effort

Giving multiple vaccines at once uses only a tiny fraction of a child's immune system capacity. Spreading vaccines out is not recommended as it leaves children unprotected for longer.

Safety & Testing Standards

All vaccines undergo long and thorough development and testing before being licensed. They are tested on adults and children separately because vaccines can work differently across age groups. No vaccines are tested on children before being fully tested on adults.
